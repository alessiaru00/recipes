Discover Gnocchi History

Gnocchi, those delightful little potato dumplings that have become a staple of Italian cuisine, have a history as rich and diverse as their flavor.
Originating in Northern Italy, the word "gnocchi" is derived from the Italian word "nocchio," meaning a knot in wood or a gnarl, reflecting the dumplings' characteristic shape.
Gnocchi quickly became a popular and economical dish, cherished for its simplicity and versatility. Over the centuries, regional variations emerged, with different areas of Italy putting
their own unique spin on the classic recipe. Whether made with flour and eggs, ricotta, or semolina, gnocchi found its way onto the tables of Italian households, each region boasting
its distinct method and preferred accompaniments.
