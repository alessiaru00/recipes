Gnocchi alla Sorrentina

Baked gnocchi with tomato sauce, mozzarella, and basil.

Indulge in the irresistible charm of Gnocchi alla Sorrentina , a classic Italian dish that captures the essence of Southern Italy.
Hailing from the picturesque Sorrento region, this dish is a celebration of simplicity, showcasing the vibrant and authentic
tastes of Italian cuisine.

Features

  • Author: Some guy in Sorrento
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Preparation: 30 min
  • Doses: 4 people
  • Average cost: €

Ingredients

  • Gnocchi: 500g
  • Tomato Sauce: 2 cups
  • Mozzarella: 200g
  • Parmesan Cheese: 30g
  • Basil: 4 leaves
  • EVO Oil: 20g
  • Salt and Pepper: to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F).

  2. Cook the potato gnocchi according to the package instructions. Usually, this involves boiling them in salted water
    until they float to the surface. Drain and set aside.

  3. Pour in the tomato sauce, season with salt and pepper, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to develop flavors.

  4. Add the cooked gnocchi to the tomato sauce, stirring gently to coat them evenly. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.

  5. Transfer the gnocchi and sauce mixture to a baking dish. Spread it out evenly.

  6. Place slices of fresh mozzarella on top of the gnocchi. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the dish.

  7. Bake in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the edges are golden.

  8. Remove from the oven and sprinkle torn fresh basil leaves over the top.

  9. Drizzle with a bit of olive oil, if desired. Serve the Gnocchi alla Sorrentina hot, and enjoy!
